# Open-Source Intelligence Methods for Analysts

Open-source intelligence relies only on publicly available information.
Disciplined analysts document every source, timestamp collection, and separate
observation from inference. Corroboration across independent sources reduces the
risk of acting on a single unverified claim.

This note covers defensive, analytical practice: verifying imagery metadata,
cross-referencing public records, and maintaining a clear chain of evidence.
Good tradecraft favors transparency and reproducibility over speed.
